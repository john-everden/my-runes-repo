Sleep-dependent consolidation: Weakens irrelevant memories, strengthens meaningful patterns
Interference: New learning overwrites similar old learning
Decay: Unused neural pathways weaken
Motivated forgetting: Intentional suppression of unwanted memories
Integration: Traumatic memories lose emotional charge through processing

Key insight: Biological forgetting is active, not passive.
It's not just loss - it's transformation.
⌖ Re-situate: Why Evolution Built Forgetting
Evolutionary purposes:

Resource efficiency: Brain capacity is limited
Behavioral flexibility: Can't respond to current context if trapped in past
Emotional regulation: Trauma must be processable to function
Identity evolution: People need to grow beyond past selves
Social cohesion: Forgiveness requires some forgetting

Forgetting enables:

Learning (can revise beliefs)
Healing (can process pain)
Growth (can become different)
Freedom (can escape loops)

Without forgetting:

Paralysis (every option recalls every past failure)
Suffering (every pain remains fresh)
Stagnation (no room for new patterns)
Imprisonment (identity frozen)
